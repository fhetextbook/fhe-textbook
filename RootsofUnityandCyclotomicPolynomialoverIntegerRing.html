A-9 Roots of Unity and Cyclotomic Polynomial over Integer Ring

In §A-7 and §A-8, we learned about the definition and properties of the μ-th roots of unity and the μ-th cyclotomic polynomial over complex numbers (i.e., X ∈ ℂ) as follows:

  • The 𝛍-th roots of unity are the solutions for Xμ = 1 over X ∈ ℂ (complex numbers). The formula for the μ-th root of unity is X = e2𝜋𝑖𝑘∕μ for all integer k such that 0 ≤k ≤μ −1.
  • The primitive 𝛍-th roots of unity (denoted as 𝛚) are those μ-th roots of unity whose order (§A-4.1) is μ (i.e., ωμ = 1 and ωμ 2 ≠1).
  • Given any primitive μ-th roots of unity ω, it can generate all primitive μ-th roots of unity by computing ωk′ such that k′ is an integer 0 < k′ < μ and 𝗀𝖼𝖽(k′,μ) = 1 (Theorem A-7.2.4 in §A-4.2).

  • The 𝛍-th cyclotomic polynomial is defined as a polynomial whose roots are the primitive μ-th roots of unity. That is,

    Φμ(x) = ∏ ω∈P(μ)(x−ω) = ∏ 0≤k≤μ−1, gcd(k,μ)=1 (x−ωk)

In this section, we will explain the μ-th cyclotomic polynomial over X ∈ ℤp (integer ring), which is structured as follows:

⟨Definition A-9⟩ Roots of Unity and Cyclotomic Polynomial over Integer Ring ℤp

  • The 𝛍-th roots of unity (denoted as 𝛚) are the solutions for Xμ ≡1 mod p. Note that these solutions are not X = ω2𝜋𝑖𝑘∕μ (the formula for the solutions over X ∈ ℂ).
  • The primitive 𝛍-th roots of unity are defined as those μ-th roots of unity whose order (§A-4.1) is μ (i.e., ωμ ≡1 mod p, and ω⌈μ 2 ⌋≢1 mod p).
  • Given any primitive μ-th roots of unity ω, it can generate all primitive μ-th roots of unity by computing ωk′ such that k′ is an integer 0 < k′ < μ and 𝗀𝖼𝖽(k′,μ) = 1.

  • The 𝛍-th cyclotomic polynomial is defined as a polynomial whose roots are the primitive μ-th roots of unity. That is,

    Φμ(x) = ∏ ω∈P(μ)(x−ω) = ∏ 0≤k≤μ−1, gcd(k,μ)=1 (x−ωk)

Note that in the μ-th cyclotomic polynomial in both cases of over X ∈ ℂ and over X ∈ ℤp, their each root ω (i.e., the primitive μ-th root of unity) has the order μ (i.e., ωμ = 1 over X ∈ ℂ, and ωμ ≡1 mod p over X ∈ ℤp). Also note that each root ω can generate all roots of the μ-th cyclotomic polynomial by computing ωk′ such that 𝗀𝖼𝖽(k′,μ) = 1.
